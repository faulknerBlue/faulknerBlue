Delaware Mines Project

Documentary Copper Mine Concert Series - 10.12.2018

The score I composed for a University of Michigan Theatre production of the Caryl Churchill play, 'Love and Information.' The play captures internet culture through a poignant lens and challenges its audience to evaluate their role in the digital age. I tried to capture the heart of the play with my music. It was my first time composing the sound design/score for a play-- it was a highly rewarding experience.

The play is divided into seven sections, and each features about 10 scenes. The scenes are brief, lasting only a couple minutes at most. 57 of these vignettes fly before the audience in an hour and a half. I found this effect to be similar to browsing through social media; in particular their 'story' features wherein users can upload ~10s highlights of their days. The 'Snapchat' texture was central to the play's sonic imprint.



Faulkner · Love and Information Soundtrack

What I did:

- Composer: Collaborated closely with faculty and student actors to compose appropriate incidental compositions

- Sound Designer: implemented appropriate recording techniques for Chinese instruments, ensured A/V synchronization

- Co-Sound Engineer: Co-engineered acoustic solutions vis a vis speaker construction and positioning for the show
